Seth MacFarlane first started as a writer for Hannah-Barbera and would go on to create his own series that would launch his career. My Profile

Seth MacFarlane was born on October 26, 1973 to Ronald Milton MacFarlane and Ann Perry in Kent, Connecticut. Seth first became interested in illustrations at a young age when he started watching cartoons and would attempt to draw them himself. \ He would go on to study film, video, & animation at the Rhode Island School of Design (RISD). While at RISD, he also performed stand-up comedy which would help him find his voice. He would eventually go on to pitch Family Guy to Fox which in turn he was offered a budget of $50,000 to produce a pilot that could potentially lead to a series. It took him a long excruciating 6 months to present the pilot to Fox and a series was immediately ordered.

Television Career

Seth's career in television first started at Hanna-Barbera working on existing shows such as Dexter's Laboratory, Cow and Chicken, and Johnny Bravo. After the success of Family Guy, he would go on to create spin offs, soundtracks, and video games based of Family Guy. In addition to other animated works like American Dad, I eventually ended staring and producing my own sci-fi comedy-drama series called The Orville.

Film Career

Seth's film career isn't as long as his tv career but it has proven to be successful nonetheless. His first film was Ted which had a budget of $50 - $65 million with a box office of $549 million. A Ted 2 would later be produced and a television series is currently in the works. The only other film in his resume is A Million Ways to Die in the West which he stared and co-wrote in 2014.

Music Career

In 2010, Seth MacFarlane signed a record deal with Universal Republic Records and released his debut album, Music is Better than Words in 2011. Alongside with multiple albums, Seth also collaborated with multiple artists such as Sara Bareilles, Norah Jones, Elizabeth Gilliese, and Ariana Grande.

The succes of the Simpsons really opened doors. It showed that if you were working in animation you didn't necessarily have to be working in kid's telivision.-- Seth MacFarlane
